What This Unit Is About

Unit 1 gives you the foundations of working in real estate in Queensland. It covers how agencies are structured, who does what, the laws you must follow, how to get your registration, and how to communicate with clients. Think of it as your "rules of the game" before you start playing.

Topic 1 — Agency Structure
🏢

What is "Agency"?

The legal relationship where a vendor or lessor (Principal) hires an agent to connect them with a buyer or tenant (Third Party). The signed document is called a PO Form 6.

Core Concept
👥

Client vs. Customer

Client = pays the agency, has a signed agreement (usually the vendor/landlord). Customer = does business with you but doesn't pay you (usually buyer/tenant).

Key Distinction
⚖️

Two Departments

Sales: Lists and sells using Exclusive, Open, or Sole Agency Agreements. Property Management: Lists and manages using Management & Leasing Agency Agreements.

Structure
🤝

Agent's #1 Obligation

Hired by the seller → always act in their best interest. Sell at the best price, shortest time, best terms. Must still be fair to buyers.

Ethics
Topic 2 — Legal Structures
🏭

Company / Corporation

Most common structure. Limited liability, separate legal identity, registered through ASIC. Directors carry significant legal responsibility.

Most Common
🧍

Sole Trader

One person owns and runs the business. All debts and liabilities are yours personally — no separation between business and personal assets.

Full Liability
👫

Partnership

Two or more people, jointly and severally liable for ALL debts — even debts you didn't know about. A legal partnership agreement is strongly advised.

Shared Liability
🛡️

Trust

Offers asset protection, tax benefits, income splitting, and succession continuity. Often used by established agencies to protect wealth.

Asset Protection

Topic 4 — Licensing & Registration
📋

Salesperson Registration

Minimum to work in real estate. Must be 18+, complete 12 units of CPP41419 Cert IV. Allows sales AND property management work. Cannot conduct auctions or operate trust accounts.

Entry Level
🏆

Real Estate Agent Licence

Required to own or run an agency. Must complete 19 units of CPP41419 Cert IV. Must be 18+ and a "fit and proper person" as assessed by OFT.

Full Licence
🔄

CPD Requirements

From June 2025, ALL agents (registration holders, licensees, letting agents, auctioneers) must complete annual Continuing Professional Development requirements.

Annual
🔑

Principal Licensee

Every place of business must have a licensed Licensee in Charge. They're responsible for all employees' actions under vicarious liability laws.

Responsibility
Topic 5 — Communication
⚡

First Impressions — 3 Seconds!

54% appearance · 39% voice tone · 7% words. Your appearance communicates before you speak. Look professional, always.

Must Know
😤

4 F's for Complaints

Fine / Feel / Felt / Found: "That's fine, I understand how you feel. Others have felt the same, and what we've found is…" Categorise as factual, misunderstanding, or unreasonable.

🏠 Real-World Scenarios

Real estate situations you might face on the job or in your assessment. Read the scenario, think about your answer, then reveal it.

Scenario 1 — Obligations

The Buyer's Best Friend

You've been working with buyers Mark and Sarah for months. You found them a great property listed by another agent. During negotiations, you start to feel sorry for them and want to push the price down as much as possible, even if it hurts the vendor. Is this correct behaviour?

❌ No! Your obligation is to the client — the vendor — who signed the PO Form 6 and pays your commission. The buyers are customers. You must be fair to them, but your primary duty is to get the best price and terms for the seller. Taking the buyer's side in negotiations is a conflict of interest.
Scenario 2 — Licensing

The Expired Registration

Jake's salesperson registration expired 6 weeks ago. He forgot to renew it but he's still showing properties and taking offers for his agency. His boss didn't notice. What's the legal situation?

⚠️ Jake is committing an offence under Section 97 of the Property Occupations Act 2014. The maximum penalty is $30,960 OR 2 years imprisonment. His expired certificate can only be restored within a 3-month restoration window at a higher cost. His agency could also face consequences under vicarious liability rules.
Scenario 3 — Legislation

The Rental Application Rejection

A landlord tells you to reject rental applications from people with children. She says "my property isn't suited for kids." What do you do?

❌ You must refuse this instruction. Rejecting tenants based on parental status is unlawful discrimination under Queensland's Anti-Discrimination Act 1991. You cannot discriminate on the basis of parental status, race, religion, gender, disability, or many other protected attributes. You must advise the landlord this is illegal and that you cannot act on this instruction.
Scenario 4 — Complaints

The Angry Tenant at the Counter

A tenant bursts into the office furious that maintenance hasn't been done for 3 weeks. Other clients are in the waiting area watching. She's raising her voice. What's your approach?

✅ First, move her to a private area away from other clients. Then use the 4 F's: "That's fine Ms [Name], I understand how you feel. Other tenants have felt the same. What we've found is we can arrange this today." Acknowledge her feelings, stay calm, don't argue. This is likely a Factual Statement complaint — something went wrong. Apologise, agree on action, and follow through.
Scenario 5 — Business Structure

The Partnership Surprise

Alex and Ben are partners in a real estate agency. Without Ben knowing, Alex takes out a loan in the partnership's name and defaults on it. Is Ben liable for the debt?

⚠️ Yes. In a partnership, both parties are "jointly and severally" liable for ALL debts — even debts one partner wasn't aware of. Ben is legally responsible for Alex's debt. This is why having a proper legal partnership agreement is critical, and why many businesses prefer a company structure with limited liability.
